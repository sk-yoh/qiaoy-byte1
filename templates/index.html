Question: Does the poverty rate of one area affect the crime rate in this area?

These are maps about US poverty rates and Dangerous city rank. We can figure out sort of relation ship between them.

My answer

There is indeed relevance between poverty rate and crime rate. For example, region of highest poverty rate like Michigan does contain couples of most dangerous cities there.

Reflection and Further thoughts

1.Dataset: the poverty rate is calculated on average by state, which can not precisely reflect the poverty rate of a small city or town. In New york state, poverty rate distributes unevenly throughout the state.
2. Metrics: What metric do they use to rank a dangerous city? Is that mostly associated with crime? I was not able to find the dataset of exact crime rate across the states and thus I use this as a substitution.
3.Time range: These 2 maps can only demonstrate the relationship at a certain time. With deeper study, it should contain the data through a relatively long period of time.
